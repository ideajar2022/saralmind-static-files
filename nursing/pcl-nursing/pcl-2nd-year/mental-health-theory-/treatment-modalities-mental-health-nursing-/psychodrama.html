Psychodrama

Psychodrama is a type of human development that examines the challenges, worries, dreams, and ultimate goals of individuals, groups, systems, and organizations via dramatic action. It is mostly employed as a group work technique in which each member of the group can act as a therapeutic intermediary for one another. The dramatic action totally engages the audience. When an audience member plays a part in the drama that supports the protagonist, they are either personally invested in the issues the protagonist, who plays the lead actor, is concerned about. In psychodrama, participants explore their inner conflicts on stage by acting out their feelings and social interactions.

Cognitive Therapy

A psychotherapy strategy known as cognitive therapy is predicated on the notion that conduct is secondary to thought. Our thoughts have an impact on our emotions and moods. Depression is brought on by thought processes that are self-defeating and demeaning of oneself. By reversing this skewed manner of thinking, feeling, and doing, the therapist aids the patient. The cognitive triad is part of the cognitive model of depression:

  • A negative view of self.
  • A negative view of environment.
  • A negative view of the future.

Aim

To treat depression, new cognitive and behavioral responses should be used to replace depressive cognition (ideas) such as hopelessness, worthlessness, helplessness, and pessimism. Because of a changed way of thinking, the person has bad feelings about themselves, expresses helplessness, despair, and a pessimistic perspective of the future, which results in inactivity, which, in turn, results in depressed mood. A prime example of a bad emotion:

  • My child is not doing well at school so; I must be a bad parent.
  • He didn't say good morning to me so he must hate me.
  • He did not say good morning to me so everyone must hate me.
  • I did not get an answer to that exam question right so I am going to fall all my exams.

Indication

  • Depression.
  • Anxiety disorder.
  • Phobic disorder.
  • Eating disorder.

Questions and Answers

What does psychodrama refers to ?

Psychodrama is a type of human development that explores the problems, issues, concerns, dreams, and highest aspirations of individuals, groups, systems, and organizations through dramatic action. It is most commonly used as a group work method in which each member of the group can act as a therapeutic agent for one another. The audience is completely immersed in the dramatic action. Audience involvement occurs either through personal interest in the concerns of the leading actor, known as the protagonist, or through participation in some drama roles that assist the protagonist.
