Overview

Psychotherapy is used to help people who have emotional issues, behavioral issues, or mental illnesses. mostly via verbal means of communication. The most common kind of psychotherapy is the patient talking about their issues with the therapist. The therapist makes an effort to comprehend the patient's issues and assist the patient in modifying upsetting ideas, feelings, or actions. The objectives of psychotherapy are to assist the patient in altering maladaptive behavior patterns, to moderate disordered behavior patterns, to assist the patient in enhancing interpersonal and other competences, to safeguard the patient's self-esteem, and to cultivate a positive outlook.

Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy is used to help people who have emotional issues, behavioral issues, or mental illnesses. mostly via verbal means of communication. The most common kind of psychotherapy is the patient talking about their issues with the therapist. The therapist makes an effort to comprehend the patient's issues and assist the patient in modifying upsetting ideas, feelings, or actions.

Lego defines psychotherapy as a form of treatment based on the means of an emotional issue, in which a qualified individual (the therapist) consciously forges a relationship of trust with the patient to:

  • Remove, modify or retard existing symptoms.
  • Mediate disturbed patterns of behaviour and/or.
  • Promote positive personality growth and development.

Goals of Psychotherapy

  • To help the patient in changing maladaptive behavior patterns.
  • To mediate disturbed patterns of behaviors.
  • To help the patient in reducing or eliminating environmental conditions that may cause such behaviour.
  • To help the patient in improving interpersonal and other competencies.
  • To protect the self-esteem of the patient.
  • To help the patient in helping the patient to resolve inner conflicts and overcome feelings of handicap.
  • To develop a positive attitude.

Phases

Ventilation/catharsis: The therapist encourages the client to relieve his emotional tensions, internal conflicts, frustration, and unhappiness by expressing his life situations openly so that the client relievers his intense feelings, during this process the therapist play as an active listener by paying positive attention and concentration.

Interpretation: The therapist assists the client to understand the link between his behavioral manifestation and the repressed intrapsychic conflicts. The therapist will analyze the behaviour of the client to formulate the diagnosis and plan of action.

Reeducation: To reduce the accumulation of tension in the unconscious mind the therapist makes the client learn the ways of solving the internal conflicts in a socially acceptable manner.

Limitation of Psychotherapy

  • It is not possible to administer for an uncooperative patient.
  • Difficult in cases of clients as to develop insight into the situation adequate intellectual capacities are essential.

The nurse giving psychotherapy should have the following qualities, she:

  • Should understand the patient's family and cultural background.
  • Should be a good listener.
  • Should be patient, sympathetic, understanding and tactful.
  • Should have an interest and concern for patients problems.
  • Should not too emotionally involved with the patient and his problems.

Questions and Answers

Define Psychotherapy ?

Psychotherapy is a treatment of individuals with emotional problems, behavioral problems or mental illness. Primarily through verbal communication. In a most type of psychotherapy, person discusses his or her problems with a therapist. The therapist tries to understand the person's problems and to help the individual change distressing thoughts, feelings or behaviors.

What are the goals of psychotherapy?
  • To assist the patient in altering unhelpful behavioral patterns.
  • To mediate problematic behavioral habits.
  • To assist the patient in removing or lowering the potential triggers for their behavior.
  • To assist the patient in developing their interpersonal and other skills.
  • To safeguard the patient's sense of worth.
  • To assist the patient in overcoming feelings of handicap and resolving internal conflicts
  • To adopt a positive outlook.
