Terms Related to Terminal Illness

Chronic Illness

A sickness that develops over time or a condition that affects human health that is persistent or has other long-lasting repercussions is referred to as a chronic ailment. When a condition has a longer than three-month duration, the word "chronic" is frequently used. Arthritis, asthma, cancer, COPD, diabetes, and viral illnesses like hepatitis C and HIV/AIDS are examples of common chronic diseases.

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), non-communicable diseases are a leading cause of death, accounting for 38 million deaths annually.

Holistic Care

All nursing practices that aim to heal the full person are often referred to as holistic nursing. A licensed nurse who practices traditional nursing with a "mind-body-spirit-emotion-environment" philosophy is known as a holistic nurse. A philosophy of life and being that is rooted on compassion, connections, and interconnectedness serves as the foundation for holistic nursing. A holistic nurse understands and applies the tenets of holistic healing to daily life and professional practice. Self-care, self-responsibility, spirituality, and introspection are all encouraged in holistic nursing.

Hospice Care

A patient with a chronic illness, a terminal illness, or a serious illness may get hospice care, which focuses on relieving their pain and symptoms while also responding to their emotional and spiritual needs. Since the 11th century, the idea of hospice has been developing in Western culture. Hospices were homes for the sick, injured, or terminally ill as well as for travelers and pilgrims back then and for generations to come in the Roman Catholic tradition. The modern definition of hospice includes both palliative care for those who are terminally ill provided in facilities like hospitals or nursing homes as well as care for those who would prefer to spend their final weeks and days at home.

Things to remember
  • A human illness or disease that is enduring or has other long-lasting effects is referred to as a chronic condition. Examples of chronic diseases include asthma, arthritis, COPD, and others.
  • The term "holistic care" refers to any nursing practice with the purpose of healing the complete patient.
  • The goal of hospice care is to relieve the pain and symptoms of seriously ill, terminally ill, or chronically ill patients while also tending to their emotional and spiritual needs.
