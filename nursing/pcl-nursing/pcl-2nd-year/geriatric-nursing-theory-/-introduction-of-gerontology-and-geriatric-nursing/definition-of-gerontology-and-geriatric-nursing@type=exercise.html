Overview

Gerontology is the study of all facets of aging, including the clinical, psychologic, economic, and sociological problems that other people face and the effects they have on both the individual and society. It combines biological, psychological, and sociologic research to examine other adults in their context. Geriatric nursing is the branch of nursing that focuses on the assessment, diagnosis, planning, implementation, and evaluation of older persons in all settings, including acute, intermediate, and skilled care as well as in the community.

Definition of Gerontology and Geriatric Nursing

Definition of gerontology and geriatric nursing.

Gerontology

Gerontology is the study of all facets of aging, such as the clinical, psychologic, economic, and sociological problems that older people face and the effects these problems have on both the individual and society. Gerontology is the interdisciplinary study of older people and their environments from biological, psychological, and sociological perspectives.

Etymology

Geras meaning ,old age and logos meaning science

Geriatric nursing

The branch of nursing that focuses on the assessment, diagnosis, planning, execution, and evaluation of older individuals in all settings, including acute, intermediate, and skilled care as well as in the community.

Things to remember
  • Study of all the aspects of aging process including clinical, psychologic,economic, and sociologic issues encountered by other persons and their consequences for both individual and society  is called gerontology.
  • The field of nursing that specializes in the field of nursing process as it relates to the assessment, diagnosis, planning, implementation and evaluation of older adults in all environment including acute,intermediate and skilled care as well as within the community is called geriatric nursing.
