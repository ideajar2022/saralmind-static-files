Introduction and Factors of Learning

Learning is a key process in human behavior or any organism. It plays a central role in the language we speak, our culture, our attitudes and belief our goals, our personality traits, our perception etc. learning can be defined any relatively permanent change in behavioral that occurs as a result of practice or experience.

Learning is not limited to acquiring new skills and academic subject matter; it also helps us to in adjustment and adoption according to environmental demand.

Factors of Effective Learning

  • Motivation
    • The learner will put forth more effort and demonstrate greater interest in learning something if their motivation is stronger and more obvious. Greater and more long-lasting learning is the end result.
  • Intelligence
    • It gives us the ability to reason, understand how things relate to one another, and make wise and critical judgments. Without intellect, learning cannot take place properly.
  • Physical maturation
    • Effective learning also requires physical development. A child, for instance, cannot learn to write without first acquiring the neuro-muscular co-ordination necessary for learning.
  • Physical condition of the learner
    • It is a significant element. A lack of energy, which is crucial for good learning, characterizes a sick person. It has been discovered that people with brain abnormalities have poor learning capacities.
  • Good working condition
    • An ideal learning environment includes things like light, air, comfort, a comfortable temperature, and the absence of distractions like noise.
  • Mental health of the learner
    • Studies have demonstrated that worries, fear, recurring nightmares, feelings of loneliness, inferiority complexes, etc. work against learning.
  • Associating things as a part of a total situation
    • The new learning should be linked to all relevant previously learned stuff. This implies that many elements of the learning environment should be interconnected.
  • Length of working period
    • The working intervals shouldn't be too long or too short. This may be a propensity to overlook the fact that the learning period is too brief while fatigue sets in and lessens learning efficiency after the learner has worked for a longer than usual period of time.

Learning is dependent on the learner's innate abilities and capacity for learning. Knowledge specifics, comprehension abilities relevant to a given field or area, learner interest, and attitude relevant to a given learning area.

What are the factors of effective learning ?
  1. Motivation: the stronger and clearer the motive in learning anything, the greater is the effort and interest shown by the learner in learning it. The result is greater and more permanent learning.
  2. Intelligence: it enables us to understand things, to see the relationship between things, to reason and judge correctly and critically. Learning cannot take place effectively without intelligence.
  3. Physical maturation: physical maturation is also necessary for effective learning. For e.g. a child cannot learn to write unless he has developed the neuro-muscular co-ordination for learning.
  4. Physical condition of the learner: it is an important factor. An unhealthy person lacks energy which is very important for effective learning. The people with abnormalities in their brain are found to have low learning capacity.
  5. Good working condition: favorable environment such as fresh air, light, comfortable surroundings, equable temperature, absence of distractions like noise etc. are a great aid in learning effectively.
  6. Mental health of the learner: experiments have shown that worries, fear, persistent day dreams, feelings of loneliness, inferiority complex etc. militates against learning.
  7. Associating things as a part of a total situation: all related material which has been learned previously should be associated with new learning. This means that different aspects of the learning situation should be connected.
  8. Length of working period: the periods of work should neither be too long nor be too short. This might be tendency to forget the learning period is too short while after the learner has worked for a longer fairly period, fatigue sets in and reduces the effectiveness of learning.
