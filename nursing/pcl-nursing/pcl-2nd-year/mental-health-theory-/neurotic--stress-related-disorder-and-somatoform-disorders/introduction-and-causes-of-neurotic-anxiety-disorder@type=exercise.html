Introduction and Causes of Neurotic-Anxiety Disorder

Patients with neurosis exhibit either a disproportionately strong or persistent emotional response to any given events or stresses. They exhibit signs of worry, fear, sadness, nebulous aches and pains, and numerous physical complaints. They are conscious of their issues and seek assistance.

In neurosis, a symptom or collection of symptoms may subjectively bother the patient without significantly impairing personality or conduct. When faced with pressures of any kind, people frequently get tight or anxious. Anxiety is one of neurosis' main symptoms.

Causes of Neurosis

Psychological theory states that the following factors lead to neurosis:

  • Internal conflict (conflict within the mind)
  • Interpersonal difficulties ( include problem in family, office, work, within the friends , etc)
  • Environmental pressure ( include loss of loved one, failure, disappointment, frustration, natural disaster, etc)
  • The personal vulnerability ( depend on upon the personality make-up of the individuals and early life experiences)

 

Things to remember
  • Neurosis are a less severe form of psychological disorder where patients show either excessive or prolonged emotional reaction to any given situations / stresses.
  • They have symptoms like anxiety, fear, sadness, vague aches/ pain and multiple physicals complains.
  • They are aware of their problems and seek help.
  • According to psychological theory, the causes of neurosis are intrapsychic conflict,interpersonal problems,the environmental stress and the individual susceptibility.
Questions and Answers

Define neurosis.

Neurosis is a less severe type of psychological disorder in which patients exhibit either an exaggerated or prolonged emotional reaction to any given situation or stress. They have symptoms such as anxiety, fear, sadness, vague aches/pain, and a variety of physical complaints. They are aware of their problems and are seeking assistance.

 

What are the causes of neurosis ?

According to psychological theory, the causes of neurosis are:

  1. Intrapsychic conflict (conflict within the mind)
  2. Interpersonal problems ( include problem in family, office, work, within the friends , etc)
  3. The environmental stress ( include loss of loved one, failure, disappointment, frustration, natural disaster, etc)
  4. The individual susceptibility ( depend on upon the personality make-up of the individuals and early life experiences)
