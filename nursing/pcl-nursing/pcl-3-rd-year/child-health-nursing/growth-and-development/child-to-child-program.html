Overview

The child-centered approach supports a child's right to decide, connect, and communicate. Children are given the freedom to independently think, experience, investigate the subject, and look for solutions. It displays an artistic celebration of kids' work. It is a novel idea in the world that healthy children should be encouraged to adopt a positive outlook on their peers and their activities. The objectives of a child-to-child program are to support the child's right to participate, to alter their feelings, knowledge, and skills rather than those of their parents, to turn bad habits into good ones, and to raise awareness of health issues.The major benefits of a child-to-child method are that it helps to improve learning in the classroom, kids and instructors may experience the excitement of learning, which has an immediate value for families and the community, and Children learn cooperation and mutual aid in this curriculum. Children's group size must be under 20, the venue must be safe and welcoming, and there must be adequate time for presentation and interaction.

Accident prevention

  • Motor vehicle accident prevention
    • Always use a vehicle seat that is well-made.
    • Keep an eye on kids when they play outside.
    • Never allow children to play on the curb or next to a parked car.
    • Do not let children play in a snowbank, mound of leaves, or cardboard box.
    • Watch people riding bicycles or tricycles.
    • Children should be taught to look for cars before crossing, identify the colors of traffic lights, and obey traffic authorities.
  • Fall prevention
    • Use a guard rail in a window and a secure nail.
    • Maintaining railings as required.
    • Place gates at the stairwell's top and bottom.
    • If a fall is possible, keep the doors shut.
    • Put carpeting in the bathroom and beneath the cot.
    • Keep kids in car seats at all times.
    • Playground, safe play space, and playground supervision.
    • Informing parents about safety precautions
  • Drowning prevention
    • When near a water source, keep a tight eye on children.
    • It is necessary to lock the gate and fence the pool.
    • Under appropriate supervision, teach water safety and how to swim.
    • Inform students of a water threat.
  • Burn prevention
    • Keep hot food, cigarettes, incense, and burning objects out of children's reach.
    • Keep tablecloths out of children's reach.
    • Keep electrical cords from appliances such as an iron out of children's reach.
    • Put plastic caps on electrical plugs as protection.
    • Explain the meaning of heat and the harm it causes.
    • Check the bath water frequently.
  • Poisoning prevention
    • All potentially dangerous substances and agents should be kept out of sight or locked.
    • Replace poisonous substances and medications right away, and replace child safety caps.
    • Don't keep many harmful compounds in storage.
    • Dispose of empty poison vials properly.
    • Never remove the labels from a harmful substance's container.
    • Find the local poison control center's phone number and address.
    • Poison storage should never be kept in the kitchen.
  • Aspiration and asphyxiation prevention
    • Avoid eating huge pieces of meat.
    • Fish, bones, dry beans, hard sweets, chewing gum, and fruit with pits should also be avoided.
    • Offer safety products without protruding edges or tiny detachable components.
    • Put dangerous items out of harm's way.
    • Close supervision and aspiration-related lessons are recommended.
  • Bodily damage prevention
    • Give children no pointy or sharp things.
    • Avoid giving candy or other comparable items to children when they are jogging or walking.
    • Impart safety advice.
    • All hazardous equipment and tools should be kept in a lockable cabinet.
    • Make parents aware of the risks posed by unattended animals.
