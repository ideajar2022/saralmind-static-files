Nepal Red Cross Society ( NRCS )

Since its establishment, THE NRCS has been involved in a variety of health-related initiatives. Health interventions have been implemented on a regular and project-specific basis.

Regular operations include ambulance service, blood, eye care, first aid, condom day, and basic health care, whereas project-based activities include community-based health programs, AID's project targeting various populations, and the eastern area family planning the enlarged project.

Mission

NRCS, guided by red cross essential principles, is devoted to providing high-quality assistance to vulnerable people by mobilizing its nationwide network of volunteers and professionals and collaborating with communities and other stakeholders.

Strategies

  1. NRCS community-based health services will be expanded and strengthened through an integrated PHC strategy.
  2. Encourage the availability of safe drinking water and sanitary facilities.
  3. Create and administer a community-based child health program that focuses on main killer diseases.
  4. Provision of contraceptive devices, both temporary and permanent.
  5. Boost HIV/AIDS preventive and control efforts.
  6. Ensure an adequate and timely supply of safe blood.
  7. Develop and implement health-in-emergency strategies as well as an appropriate service package in collaboration with the relevant NRCS functions.

Major Services

  1. Ambulance services
    Since 1964, the NRCS has provided regular ambulance service as one of its key components.
  2. Blood transfusion service
    The Nepal government has handed NRCS sole authority over the country's blood program. There are 68 blood transfusion centers and 25 hospital units in the city.
  3. Eye care service
    Aside from other health-related initiatives, an institutional and community-based method has been used to provide primary eye care services primarily in the Janakpur, Bheri, and Karnali zones.
  4. HIV & AIDS prevention, care, and support program
    In five districts, the Red Cross is developing an HIV prevention, care, and support program. The emphasis has been on preventing additional infection, expanding care, treatment, and support for HIV-positive people and affected communities, and reducing HIV-related stigma and discrimination.
  5. First aid services
    One of the primary functions of the NRCS is to provide first aid services. In both rural and urban communities, the service is offered by NRCS trainers and volunteers from district chapters, sub-chapters, youth circles, and the cooperation committee.

Questions and Answers

What is the concept of Nepal Red Cross Society (NRCS)?

Since its inception, The NRCS has been executing numerous health-related initiatives. Health intervention has been put into practice on a regular basis and through targeted projects.

Regular activities include ambulance service, blood donation, eye care, first aid, condom distribution, and primary healthcare, whereas project-based activities include AID's project targeting different groups, the expansion of family planning services in the eastern region, and community-based health programs.

What are the Strategies of NRCS?
  • NRCS community-based health services should be strengthened and expanded using an integrated PHC strategy.
  • Encourage the availability of sanitary facilities and clean drinking water.
  • Create and implement a community-based program for children's health that focuses on the treatment of serious fatal illnesses.
  • Provision of both long-term and short-term contraceptive methods.
  • Bolster prevention and control efforts for HIV/AIDS.
  • Ensure a timely and sufficient supply of healthy blood.
  • In conjunction with the relevant NRCS functions, create and implement health in emergency strategies and the corresponding service package.

What are the major services of NRCS?
  • Ambulance services
    • One of the main aspects of NRCS's regular service since 1964 is its ambulance service.
  • Blood transfusion service
    • The Nepali government has given NRCS the sole authority to manage the nation's blood program. There are 25 hospital units and 68 blood transfusion facilities.
  • Eye care service
    • Along with other health-related initiatives, a community-based and institutional approach has been used to provide primary eye care services primarily in the zones of Janakpur, Bheri, and Karnali.
  • HIV & AIDS prevention , care and support program
    • In five districts, the Red Cross is developing an HIV prevention, care, and support program. The main goals have been to stop new infections, increase care, support, and treatment for those who have HIV and the communities they live in, and lessen stigma and discrimination associated with the disease.
  • First aid services
    • One of the main functions of the NRCS is to provide first aid services. In both rural and urban communities, the service is delivered by NRCS trainers and volunteers of district chapters, sub-chapters, youth circles, and cooperation committees.
