Peace Corps, Paropakar, and USAID

Peace Corps

 Peace Corps was founded in 1961 in Washington, DC. It is a non-profit organization that sends trained volunteers to developing nations by  focusing on economic and social progress is central to this work. Volunteers in the Peace Corps are all U.S. citizens with at least a bachelor's degree who commit to serving their country overseas for a full two years.

There are three goals that make up the Peace Corps' state mission which includes;

  • To assist interested countries in meeting their demand for trained men and women.
  • To promote a greater understanding of Americans among those served.
  • To encourage Americans to have a greater understanding of other people.

 

Paropakar

Paropakar Sanstha is a nonprofit group whose name comes from the Sanskrit words for "doing good for others" (paro) and "order" (santha). This is the earliest known group in Nepal. A product of 2004 BS, its inception can be traced back to Dayabir Singh Kansakar.

 

The primary objective of Paropakar Sanstha was to provide malaria, dysentery, cholera, and typhoid medication to the poor. There was an expansion of Paropakar Sanstha's services in many areas of social welfare after the country's democracy was restored in 2007 BS.

Paropakar Sanstha also provides a number of other community services which includes;
 

Other social services offered by Paropakar Sanstha include:

  1. Emergency medical services
  2. Programs for blood donation
  3. A donation department has been established in several districts of Nepal, from which free medicines are supplied.
  4. First-aid stations can be found in a variety of locations.
  5. Festivals, public fairs, and religious ceremonies such as Shiva Ratri, Krishnashthami, and so on are examples of times when the social organization takes an active role.

USAID (United States Agency for International Development)

The United States Agency for International Development plays a pivotal role in formulating and enforcing U.S. global health policy. It is an independent federal agency that promotes the economic and political interests of the United States abroad through economic development and humanitarian assistance.

The United States Agency for International Development (USAID) was established in 1961 to advance international progress. Countries in Africa, Asia, Latin America, and beyond have all benefited from it.

The agency works in five major areas:

  1. Increasing economic growth.
  2. Democracies are being advanced.
  3. Providing humanitarian aid to victims of famine and other natural disasters.
  4. Defending public health and promoting family planning
  5. Environmental protection.

 

Things to remember
  • Peace crops is an independent United States government organization founded in 1961 and headquartered in Washington, DC.

  • The work is primarily concerned with social and economic development.
  • Paropakar means "doing well for others," and Santha means "order." As a result, Paropakar Sanstha is a charitable organization.
