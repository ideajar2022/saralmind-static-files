Information Management System

A system for managing an organization is an information management system. a system that delivers the necessary data to each management level at the appropriate time, in the appropriate format, and in the desired amount and quality so that it may serve as the foundation for decision-making. An information system used for decision-making, as well as for the coordination, control, analysis, and visualization of information inside an organization, is known as a management information system.

People, procedures, and technology are all examined in the study of management information systems within an organizational setting. In a corporate context, using a management information system is ultimately intended to boost the enterprise's worth and bottom line.

Data from every department is integrated into the information system. It benefits and gives operations and management the data they need. The system that converts data into information useful in supporting decision-making might be computer-based or manual.
